Expert Roof Pressure Washing – The Soft Wash Way

Roof pressure washing can dislodge twigs, seeds, leaves, and other debris from under shingles and tiles, but a high-pressure roof power washing can also damage those shingles, even blowing them right off the roof! A soft wash roof cleaning uses high-quality detergents and surfactants to dissolve dirt and debris, and less water pressure to rinse away that waste, so that roofing shingles don’t get dislodged or otherwise damaged.

A soft wash roof cleaning is also best for older roofs, or those with damaged or brittle tiles and shingles. A high-pressure wash, or scraping a roof with a stiff-bristled brush, can damage those weakened tiles, causing them to become thin and crumbly. Thin, worn, or crumbly roofing tiles increases the risk of water leaks into the home, and also means losing more heat and air conditioning through the roof, causing your utility bills to spike!

Why a Soft Wash is Best for Cleaning Moss Off Roof

Moss, algae, mold, and mildew can grow between roofing tiles, causing those shingles to become loose, so they’re more likely to blow away in a storm. A high-pressure roof cleaning may not effectively remove this debris, as pressurized water only blows away their vegetation, without killing their spores!

Soft wash roof cleaning chemicals are designed to remove biological materials, including the spores of moss, algae, and other such contaminants. Having a soft wash roof cleaning for an Westchester home means less risk of that moss, algae, mold, mildew, and other irritants growing back over the years.

Your Roof Cleaning Cost Is An Investment In Your Home

Regular roof cleaning of a Michigan home is an investment in your property. A thorough and professional roof wash will protect shingles from damage caused by acid rain residue, twigs that scrape the surface of those tiles, and moss that loosens tiles from their connectors. You then need to replace those shingles far less often when you invest in a regular roof soft wash for your home.

Roof cleaning of a Michigan home also removes contaminants that hold moisture next to the roof paper, causing it to get soft and crumbly. When roofing paper gets soft, it’s more prone to suffering rips or tears, increasing the risk of water leaks and resultant mold growth in the home. A roof soft wash then protects your home’s roof from this damage.

Roof Pressure Washing Is Not a DIY Job!

If your home needs roof cleaning, why not just rent a pressure washer and tackle this job yourself? The short answer is that a roof wash is not a DIY job! One important reason for this is that misusing a pressure washer can mean blowing tiles and shingles right off your home’s roof or causing old and brittle tiles to crumble and break away.

It can also be challenging to decide on the right roof cleaning chemicals to use for a home’s roof, as some cleansers are meant for just a lightweight cleaning, whereas other, stronger chemicals are needed to kill mold, algae, mildew, and moss. However, these stronger chemicals can be hazardous and harmful to your skin, eyes, and nasal passages, and can also harm your precious flowerbeds and landscaping features. To protect your property and even your health, leave this work to a professional roof cleaning company.

Trained technicians from a roof cleaning company will also know how to stay safe on ladders or on the roof itself. Properly trained technicians will also know when a soft wash roof cleaning is recommended, to protect old and brittle tiles, or to ensure that mold, moss, algae, and other biological contaminants are thoroughly cleaned and washed away from your home’s roof!
